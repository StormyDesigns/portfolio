Overview

Keeping borrowers engaged in a competitive market

Tala is an Android application serving emerging markets — Philippines, Kenya, Mexico, and India — offering immediate credit access, quick loans, and financial accounts. Users apply for loans and gain access to checking accounts and bill pay upon approval.

I was the sole designer on Tala Rewards, an in-app loyalty program designed to incentivize borrowers to engage in positive financial behaviors. I worked alongside a product manager, researcher, product marketing manager, engineers, graphic designers, and a systems designer.

The Problem

Tala was losing users to competitors

Competing apps were pulling users away by offering lower transfer fees, higher loan utility, rewards and referral programs, and reduced friction for accessing funds.

Internal data revealed a key insight: users who engaged with multiple Tala features showed significantly higher repayment rates and longer retention. The opportunity was to make multi-feature engagement feel rewarding — not just functional.

Tala's big bet: Multi-product engagement through incentivized actions drives higher lifetime value via stronger repayment rates and retention.

Proof of Concept

Testing the water

Before committing to the full program, I partnered with the product manager and researcher to test whether incentivizing account deposits through rewards would actually move behavior. It did — we saw a 30% increase in account deposits. That result gave us the confidence to invest in a full rewards system.

Research

Competitive analysis

I examined loyalty program apps in both the Philippines and the US to identify common patterns and user expectations. Four key themes emerged: prominent display of points or status, a mix of quick and long-term rewards, variety to prevent boredom, and clear guides explaining how to earn tokens.

Design Process

User flow

I created a user flow mapping out the full experience of navigating through the rewards feature — from entry points to redemption. Once reviewed and approved by the product manager, engineers, and design team, I moved into screen designs.

Design Explorations

Iterations 1 & 2

My first iteration placed the token amount and progress bar at the top, with a "View rewards" button, an activity log, a "How to earn" section, and a list of earnable activities. The second iteration nested rewards and activities behind earn/redeem buttons to reduce visual overwhelm.

For reward options, I separated rewards by token milestone. A disabled redeem button appeared when the user didn't have enough tokens, with a dedicated page for reward details and redemption confirmation.

Problems identified

After review, several issues surfaced: unclear information hierarchy, nested rewards obscuring the incentive, a fixed 400-token cap the PM wanted removed, reward cards taking up too much space, and no celebratory moment on redemption.

Refined Design

Designs for user testing

I tested two entry point placements — one in the bottom navigation and one at the top of the screen — to see which was more noticeable.

I added an onboarding introduction screen before the user enters the rewards home page, using the opportunity to clearly communicate what Tala Rewards is and how to earn.

User Testing

Moderated usability tests with 14 users

I conducted moderated usability tests with 14 users segmented by borrowing history to test two hypotheses:

Hypothesis 1

  • Users understand how to earn tokens
  • Users understand how to redeem rewards
  • Users grasp the token-to-reward relationship

Hypothesis 2

  • Users feel motivated to complete desired behaviors
  • Rewards feel appealing and worth the effort

Mature borrowers (n=7)

  • 10+ loans disbursed
  • Mixed transaction history

Casual borrowers (n=7)

  • 1–3 loans disbursed
  • Mixed transaction history

Both hypotheses validated ✓

Users clearly understood the activity-to-token mechanics and could identify redeemable vs. unredeemable rewards. They also understood that tokens would be deducted upon redemption.

On motivation: non-transacting users said they were likely to try new features for rewards. Transacting users said they'd increase their engagement. Local rewards — Jollibee, supermarkets, drugstores — drove the strongest motivation.

Post-Testing Updates

Aligning with the design system

After testing, I collaborated with the brand design team and systems designer to align the final designs with Tala's newly finalized design system. One meaningful terminology change came out of this phase: "Tokens" became "Points" — users were more familiar with points from other apps, which improved immediate comprehension.

Reflection

Key learnings

Navigating ambiguity in an unfamiliar market. I couldn't rely on personal knowledge of the Philippines market, so I leaned heavily on the local researcher — and that partnership was essential. It reinforced how much good research partnerships matter when designing for a context you haven't lived in.

Contributing to a design system mid-project. Working without a finalized system was a constraint, but it also created an opportunity. By collaborating closely with the brand and systems teams throughout, I was able to contribute components and patterns that outlasted the project itself.
